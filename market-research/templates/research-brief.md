## 4. Pyramid Analysis Plan

Our research will follow the four-level pyramid model:

### Level 4 (Bottom): Segment & Market Analysis

**Research questions:**
- What is the total addressable market size (TAM)?
- What is the serviceable addressable market (SAM)?
- What is the serviceable obtainable market (SOM)?
- What is the market CAGR and key growth drivers?
- What are the primary market trends affecting growth?
- What market segments exist?

**Priority sources:**
- [List specific databases, reports, or agencies]

**Expected output:**
- Market sizing model (top-down and bottom-up)
- Trend analysis with supporting data
- Segment identification and sizing

### Level 3: Customer Insights

**Research questions:**
- Who are the customers? (Segmentation)
- What are their primary needs and pain points?
- What motivates their purchase decisions?
- What barriers prevent purchase or adoption?
- What is the decision-making process?
- What are their preferences and priorities?

**Priority sources:**
- [List specific surveys, academic research, consumer reports]

**Expected output:**
- Customer segment profiles (3-5 segments)
- Needs assessment matrix
- Decision journey map
- Barrier analysis

### Level 2: Competitive Landscape

**Research questions:**
- Who are the top 3-5 competitors?
- How are they positioned?
- What is their market share?
- How do they communicate and differentiate?
- How do they address customer needs?
- What are their strengths and vulnerabilities?

**Priority sources:**
- [List company sources, analyst reports, news databases]

**Expected output:**
- Competitor profiles (top 5)
- Positioning map
- Competitive matrix
- Share analysis

### Level 1 (Top): Your Company Position

**Research questions:**
- What is the current strategy and business model?
- What are core organizational capabilities?
- What are unique strengths and advantages?
- How does culture support or hinder strategy?
- What is the brand positioning potential?
- What gaps exist vs. market opportunities?

**Priority sources:**
- [Internal documents + external benchmarking sources]

**Expected output:**
- Capability assessment
- SWOT analysis
- Strategic fit analysis
- Gap identification

### Quality Standards
- [ ] All sources CRAAP tested (score ≥18 for primary evidence)
- [ ] Methodology reviewed for all quantitative claims
- [ ] Geographic and industry relevance confirmed
- [ ] Publication dates within appropriate timeframe
- [ ] Bias and purpose evaluated for all sources

### Confidence Levels
We will assign confidence levels to all findings:
- **High confidence:** 3+ independent Tier 1-2 sources converge
- **Medium confidence:** 2 independent sources align, some methodology limitations
- **Low confidence:** Single source or divergent sources, stated with caveats
