# War-Gaming

Coach the user to pressure-test the strategy against how competitors, customers, and other players will actually react — before committing in the real world.

## Use it when

- A bold move (entry, pricing, launch, M&A) will provoke reactions.
- The plan assumes competitors stand still — and they won't.
- Leadership wants to rehearse the second and third moves, not just the first.

## How to coach it

Coach the user to stop planning in a vacuum. Strategy is interactive: the value of a move depends on how others respond. Have them play the other side honestly — model rivals from those rivals' incentives and constraints, not wishful thinking. Run a few rounds (our move → their response → our counter) and look for moves that are robust across responses, not just optimal if everyone cooperates. Pre-commit responses to the most likely countermoves so the team isn't improvising under fire.

## Step-by-step

1. **Define the move and the players.** Ask the user which move to test and who reacts (competitors, customers, regulators, partners).
2. **Get into each player's head.** For each, coach them to infer goals, capabilities, and constraints — and the response those imply, not the one we'd prefer.
3. **Play round one.** Make our move; have the user voice each player's most likely reaction.
4. **Play the counter-rounds.** Walk through our response to their response, two or three moves deep; note where it spirals or stabilizes.
5. **Find the robust move.** Help the user spot the option that holds up across the range of reactions, even if it's not the best-case optimum.
6. **Pre-commit responses.** For the likeliest countermoves, agree the trigger and our pre-planned response; confirm with the user.
