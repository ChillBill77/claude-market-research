# Risk & Mitigation

Coach the user to surface what could break the strategy, judge each risk honestly, and decide what to do about the few that matter.

## Use it when

- A plan is taking shape and needs stress-testing before commitment.
- Leadership asks "what could go wrong?" and deserves a real answer.
- A go/no-go where the downside needs to be understood, not hand-waved.

## How to coach it

Help the user think like a skeptic without becoming paralyzed. Push them past generic risks ("execution risk") to specific, describable failure modes. Rate by likelihood and impact so attention goes to the few that are both probable and damaging — not the long tail. For each serious risk, insist on a real response (avoid / reduce / transfer / accept) and an early-warning signal, so the plan can react before the risk fully lands.

## Step-by-step

1. **Surface the risks.** Ask the user where the plan is most fragile — across market, execution, financial, organizational, external. Coach them to be specific about *how* each fails.
2. **Rate likelihood × impact.** Walk each risk through both; help them resist rating everything "high".
3. **Focus on the vital few.** Pull out the risks that are both likely and damaging; set the rest aside as "monitor".
4. **Choose a response.** For each vital risk, coach the user to pick avoid / reduce / transfer / accept — and say concretely what that means.
5. **Define early warnings.** Ask what signal would tell them the risk is materializing in time to act; assign who watches it.
6. **Confirm residual risk.** Summarize the risk picture after mitigations and confirm the user is comfortable proceeding.

## Deliverable

```markdown
# Risk & Mitigation — [strategy/plan]

## Risk register
| Risk (how it fails) | Likelihood | Impact | Priority |
|---|---|---|---|

## Responses (vital few)
| Risk | Response (avoid/reduce/transfer/accept) | Action | Early-warning signal | Owner |
|---|---|---|---|---|

## Residual risk
[What remains after mitigation, and why it's acceptable]
```
